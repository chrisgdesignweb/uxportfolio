CapeShare iOS App

CapeShare is the quintessential insider's guide to Cape Cod's finest attractions. Offering a complete spectrum of choice and price range, the majority of the focus is on quality, local businesses that cater to each town.

Design Role

  • UX Design
  • Visual Design

Deliverables

  • Personas
  • Wireframes
  • Visual Design
  • Assets for Dev and App Store

Problem

Planning your ultimate vacation requires a lot of time and effort. Reading reviews, and searching destination attractions can seem daunting. Finding the best takes time and effort.

Solution

CapeShare is for those folks who wish to find a quick and easy way to experience the best Cape Cod has to offer. This app is an insider's view of the best restaurants, beaches, outdoor activities, arts and culture and rainy day activities.

User Research

We surveyed potential users on frustrations they have when traveling to a new location.

80%

of survey respondents said that Google is their number one place to find information before traveling to a new destination.

65%

of survey respondents said they were not satisified with the tools they used keep track of research on a location before you visit.

70%

of survey respondents said that local attractions are the most important information to plan for before visiting a new location.

User Personas



Janice, The Working Mother

  • Age: 35 Years old
  • Education: College

"I'm taking my family on a trip to Cape Cod and want to make sure that we can explore the best that it has to offer. I don't have time to weed through Google to find everything."

User Goals
  • Find great beaches and restaurants for my family.
  • Save ideas for things to do inside of one app.
  • Have more fun with less planning.
Frustrations & Pain Points
  • Waeeding through Google to find things to do on vacation.
  • Not getting the most current information on Cape Cod.
  • Technology making her life more complex rather than saving time.

Jonathan, Student

  • Age: 21 Years old
  • Education: College

"I use technology to help make my life easier and also impress those around me. When I travel, I want to be the one in my group that knows of the latest/coolest spots in town"

User Goals
  • Find cool breweries, restaurants and beaches before going on the trip.
  • Save great ideas for our group.
  • Impress my social circle with my local knowledge of things to do.
Frustrations & Pain Points
  • Googling everything before the trip is time consuming.
  • Traditional media doesn't contain the latest and greatest.
  • Outdated apps without current information.

Wireframes

Styleguide

Working closely with a mobile development team we wanted to make sure that the handoff of assets went as smooth as possible. We setup a styleguide to help improve the communication between teams.

UI Design and Final Product

Branding At Local Attractions

In order to build community around our application we handed out stickers to all of the vendors featured in our application. This helped our businesses to be easily spotted but also built interest around the application.

Lessons Learned

I learned alot from designing this mobile app. What separates this from other mobile apps I've designed, is that I was immersed in every step of the process - from creating an idea, to launching the final product in the Apple Store. Working closely with a mobile development team in Boston as both a designer and project manager helped me to fully understand how each role contributes to the success of the app.
