AnswerRocket is an AI-driven data analytics platform that enables users to pose natural language questions about their data and interact with the results. The platform provides engaging insights through a newsfeed format, optimized for mobile device consumption. Following the launch of the mobile application, downloads increased over the subsequent months as our services team demonstrated the NewsFeed feature to both current and prospective enterprise customers.

My Role

I was the Sr. Designer on the project tasked with holding design workshops, wireframing, UI design and usability testing.

Design Workshops with Stakeholders

I was tasked by our Head of Product to organize brainstorming sessions with the product team to generate ideas for adapting the NewsFeed from the desktop experience to mobile devices. Utilizing our personas, we conducted two working sessions to address the key issues involved in this transition.

Discussions and work around ways to drill into the data and exploring social features in a wireframe session.

Content Hierarchy

As a team, we were still struggling with the volume of information coming from the data and being presented. I thought it might be helpful to organize a Hierarchy of Information Workshop with stakeholders to surface some good insights.

Here I am working with the Product Team to understand based on their experience what information is most important for our persona to see within a mobile experience. They rank the items coming out the data and we discuss as a team.

Early UI Design Explorations

Some of the early UI explorations that were shared with stakeholders for feedback.

Final UI Design Solutions

We reviewed the information hierarchy, created UI screens, and added social aspects to the newsfeed. After auditing various social UI and UX patterns, we decided to focus on enabling comments with @mentions to encourage user engagement.

Remote Usability Testing

During the pandemic, I conducted usability testing using Lookback on 5 participants' devices. I also involved a PM in a testing session to validate our assumptions and enhance UX maturity in the organization, which was successful. Results of Testing:

80%

Used search to find sections and headlines.

100%

Wanted better access to the table to view their data. This is the view they work in mostly.

80%

Of users wanted to swipe between screens rather than use the back buttons.

60%

Not clear that the pasta ebro report was a report, most thought it was more of a folder in the data story.

100%

Were confused about what ‘Feedback’ was for - the data story or giving feedback to AR.

100%

Really liked the social features and would certainly use them when looking at a data story that might interest a colleague.

100%

Tried to swipe down to remove comments, which was not available

60%

Believed search is best way to navigate the feed.

Working with Engineering

We collaborated with engineering to address the findings from usability testing. We revamped the entire mobile application and aligned the front end with our Design System, Galaxy, which I created and introduced within the company.

As a Design System Advocate, I spearheaded the creation of the Galaxy Design System to increase the efficiency of the Dev team and and reduced design maintenance costs.
